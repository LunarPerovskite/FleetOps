# FleetOps Monetization Strategy

## Philosophy

**Open core, paid hosting.** FleetOps is and will always be open source. Revenue comes from convenience, not restrictions.

## Revenue Streams

### 1. FleetOps Cloud (SaaS) — Primary

Managed hosting for organizations that don't want to self-host.

| Plan | Price | Features |
|------|-------|----------|
| **Free** | $0/mo | 3 agents, 1 team, 1GB logs, community support |
| **Pro** | $29/mo | 10 agents, 3 teams, 10GB logs, email support |
| **Business** | $99/mo | Unlimited agents, 10 teams, 100GB logs, priority support, SLA |
| **Enterprise** | Custom | Dedicated infrastructure, SSO, audit support, custom contracts |

### 2. Professional Services

- **Implementation**: $5k-50k depending on org size
- **Training**: $500/person
- **Custom integrations**: $150/hr
- **Audit/compliance consulting**: Project-based

### 3. Marketplace (Future)

- Premium connectors (Salesforce, SAP, etc.)
- Agent templates/workflows
- Custom dashboard themes
- Revenue share with creators (70/30)

### 4. Support Tiers

| Tier | Price | Response Time |
|------|-------|--------------|
| Community | Free | Best effort |
| Standard | $99/mo | 24hr business days |
| Priority | $499/mo | 4hr business days |
| Enterprise | Custom | 1hr 24/7 |

## Why This Works

1. **No vendor lock-in**: Self-hosted option always free
2. **Value alignment**: We succeed when customers succeed
3. **Community growth**: Open source attracts contributors
4. **Enterprise trust**: Transparent codebase = trust

## Free Tier Sustainability

- Limits are generous but real
- Conversion to paid ~5-10%
- Cloud hosting costs offset by paid tiers
- Community support reduces costs

## Open Source Commitment

- Core governance engine: always open
- All connectors: open source
- Documentation: open
- Roadmap: public GitHub projects

## Paid-Only Features (Cloud Only)

- Managed infrastructure
- Auto-scaling
- Advanced analytics
- Custom domains
- Priority support

Note: All features available in self-hosted version. Cloud value = convenience, not exclusivity.
